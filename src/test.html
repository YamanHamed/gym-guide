🔥 Push / Pull / Legs (PPL) intermediate

🎯 Goal: Hypertrophy & Strength ⚡ Frequency: 2x per muscle group / week
📆 Days needed
3–6 days / week
Core PPL runs 6 days (3 on / 1 off) or 3–4 day variant
🎯 Muscle targeted
Push: chest, shoulders, triceps
Pull: back, biceps, rear delts | Legs: quads, glutes, hams, calves
⏱️ Session duration
50–75 min
Depends on volume & rest periods
🧠 Experience level
Intermediate to Advanced
Beginners can use modified 3-day PPL
✅ Best for
High frequency & balanced upper/lower
Hitting each muscle twice weekly
⚠️ Drawbacks
Requires 5–6 days commitment
May be fatiguing for new lifters
📅 Sample weekly schedule (6-day PPL)
Day Focus Example exercises
Day 1 Push A (Chest/Shoulders/Tris) Barbell bench press, OHP, triceps rope pushdown, lateral raises
Day 2 Pull A (Back/Biceps) Pull-ups, barbell rows, seated cable row, dumbbell curls
Day 3 Legs A (Quad focus) Squats, leg press, walking lunges, calf raises
Day 4 Push B (Incline/triceps) Incline DB press, dips, overhead triceps extension, front raises
Day 5 Pull B (Deadlift/horizontal pull) Deadlifts, face pulls, hammer curls, lat pulldowns
Day 6 Legs B (Posterior chain) Romanian deadlifts, hamstring curls, hip thrusts, goblet squats
Day 7 Rest / Active recovery Light cardio, mobility or full rest

💡 Alternate: run PPL as 3 days/week (push/pull/legs once weekly) + 2 accessory days.

💪 Progressive overload & recovery — success tips

  • ✔️ Add 2–5 lbs or 1–2 reps each week on main lifts
  • ✔️ Track your workouts (app or notebook) to avoid plateaus
  • ✔️ Start with 2–3 working sets per exercise, adjust volume based on recovery
  • ✔️ Prioritize form before ego lifting → prevents injury
  • ✔️ Deload every 6–8 weeks: reduce intensity by 40–50%
  • ✔️ Sleep 7–9h & eat enough protein (~1.6-2.2g/kg bodyweight)
🏋️ Other splits: Upper/Lower (4 days) • Full Body (3 days) • Bro Split (5 days)
📌 Customization: Swap leg days order, add arm focus, or combine shoulders with legs for flexibility.
